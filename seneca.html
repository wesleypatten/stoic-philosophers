Seneca

Seneca was a Roman Stoic philosopher, statesman, and playwright. His works focus on ethics, resilience, and self-mastery. As an advisor to Emperor Nero, he sought to promote wisdom and virtue through his writings, including Letters to Lucilius.

Notable Quotations

  • Luck is what happens when preparation meets opportunity.
  • We suffer more often in imagination than in reality.
  • It is not that we have a short time to live, but that we waste a lot of it.
  • He who is brave is free.
  • Begin at once to live, and count each separate day as a separate life.
